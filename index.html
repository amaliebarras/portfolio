Once upon a time, I had a seriously bad relationship with numbers



This was partially due to a series of impatient teachers in primary school, and partially due to a widely held belief that a creative person could not also be a numbers person. In 2009, I applied to advertising school with big plans to become a designer, and I was super excited, but not about the required digital measurement classes. Out of necessity, I cracked open this book called Web Analytics 2.0
AND IT ROCKED MY WORLD
Because finally someone was talking to me ...about numbers
Like a REAL PERSON
And I understood it
And I wanted more

*cue identity crisis*

When I shared with my prof how life-changing it was to be related-to instead of talked-at, he introduced me to the world of data visualization. I became fascinated with the work of Kim Rees, Hans Rosling, Nathan Yau, Nick Felton...You’re telling me I can work with data AND use my design chops to help us non-nerds understand it?

I wanted to shout it from the mountaintops, but that’s not where decisions get made, so I took to starting my career as an analyst, knowing I had the secret weapons:

storytelling and visualization

Now that I’ve found my place in the sun, let me help you:

Analyze your data
Tell stories with it
Design your data platform’s UX
Develop your website
Remember to breathe

Let me help you make “numbers people” out of your people

But first, let me show you what I can do
